Research Interests

Carbene Transfer Reactions

Development of metal-catalyzed and photochemical carbene transfer strategies for C–H functionalization, cyclopropanation, and ylide formation using diazo compounds as carbene precursors.

NHC Catalysis & Asymmetric Synthesis

Design and application of thiazolium and other N-heterocyclic carbene (NHC) catalysts for enantioselective radical and ionic transformations, including acyl radical reactions.

Photochemical Synthesis

Harnessing visible light to access reactive intermediates and enable transformations — including O–H functionalization, ring expansions, and cross-coupling — under mild conditions.

C–H Functionalization

Direct functionalization of C–H bonds in heterocycles, arenes, and aliphatic systems using gold-, rhodium-, and iron-catalyzed carbene and nitrene transfer reactions.

Ylide Rearrangements

Mechanistic and synthetic investigation of [1,2]- and [2,3]-sigmatropic rearrangements of sulfonium, selenonium, and oxonium ylides generated via metal-carbene pathways.

Reaction Mechanism & Theory

Combining experimental kinetic studies with computational methods (DFT, energy decomposition analysis) to elucidate mechanisms and guide the rational design of new transformations.
